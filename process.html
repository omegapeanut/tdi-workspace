Process / 01 — Methodology

Six stages, one accountable team.

Every TDI project follows the same disciplined sequence — from a blank room to a working space. No surprises, no hand-offs, no finger-pointing. Here's what happens at each stage, who's responsible, and what you receive.

01

Discovery & brief‑setting.

Week 1 – 2 · Free of charge

We start in your space, not ours. Two facilitated workshops with leadership and end‑users surface how your people actually work — their rhythms, friction points, and the rituals that hold the culture together.

You leave with a written brief, a benchmarked square‑footage target, and a feasibility study against your timeline and budget.

Lead
Design Director
Deliverables
Written brief · Feasibility · Programme
02

Concept & strategic options.

Week 3 – 6

Three concept directions, each modelled against your brief — usually a baseline, a stretch, and a contrarian. We test them in plan, in materials, and in cost, then converge on one direction with you.

By the end of this stage, you've seen the space three different ways and chosen the one that fits.

Lead
Design Director · Senior Designer
Deliverables
3 concepts · Material boards · Indicative cost
03

Design development.

Week 7 – 12

The chosen concept gets resolved to construction quality — floor plans, RCPs, joinery sections, lighting plans, and FF&E procurement. M&E gets coordinated against the architectural intent before tender.

We hold three formal client reviews in this stage; the last is your go/no‑go decision before site.

Lead
Senior Designer · M&E Coordinator
Deliverables
Drawing set · Specifications · Fixed cost
04

Documentation & tender.

Week 11 – 14 · Overlapping

We package the design set into a BCA‑compliant submission and the tender pack. On design‑build engagements, we go straight to a fixed‑price proposal under our own contract.

You receive a single quantified bill of materials — no "provisional sums" to creep on later.

Lead
Project Manager · Documentation
Deliverables
BCA package · Tender · Fixed BoQ
05

Construction & delivery.

Programme varies

Our own site supervisors run the build, with the design team on site weekly. In‑house joinery means feature work arrives on a truck, not from a third‑party. You get a weekly programme report, real‑time cost dashboard, and a single point of contact.

If something slips, you hear about it that week — not at handover.

Lead
Director of Delivery
Deliverables
Weekly reports · Snag walks · Live cost
06

Handover & aftercare.

Last 2 weeks + 12 mo

Pre‑occupancy snag walk with you, full O&M handover, and a 12‑month defects liability period built into every contract. We come back at six months for a workspace audit — usage data, defects review, and any tuning.

If a hinge fails in month eleven, that's still on us.

Lead
Project Manager · Workshop
Deliverables
O&M docs · 12mo DLP · 6mo audit
— 02   What's different

Three things most firms won't do.

/ I

One contract, one team

Design and build under one roof. Intent doesn't get lost in translation, and accountability doesn't get diffused across two parties.

/ II

Real cost transparency

Weekly cost reports, no provisional sums, fixed bills of materials. You always know the number you're committing to next week.

/ III

Aftercare that lasts

A 12‑month DLP, six‑month workspace audit, and a single named contact for the life of the building. Most firms walk away at handover.
