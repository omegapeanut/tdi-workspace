— 01   The Brief

Consolidate four floors of live trading without dropping a single quote.

The client — a top‑10 global investment bank — had grown across four non‑contiguous floors in three different buildings. Trading was live across all of them. The mandate was to consolidate into a single 22,000 sqft Marina Bay tenancy, opening with their regional CEO on stage at the new client suite, fourteen months from kick‑off.

The variables that couldn't move were unforgiving: a quarter‑end blackout window every three months that made any disruption impossible, a 38‑millisecond network latency target between trading floors and the data centre, and a Class‑A acoustic specification for the client meeting suite that sat directly above the cafeteria. Everything else was negotiable. We had to choose carefully.

— 04   Solutions

Three constraints. Three architectural moves.

1. Quarter‑end blackouts as design constraint, not afterthought.

Rather than schedule around blackout windows, we made them the planning grid. The full programme was sliced into four three‑month build packages, each scoped to end at a blackout. Anything that couldn't fit a package didn't make the cut — and that ruthlessness drove decisions that improved the design.

"They handed over four weeks ahead of programme — under budget and without a single defect at snag walk."

2. A separate building inside the building.

The client suite was specified as a tenancy‑within‑a‑tenancy. Independent acoustic envelope, separate M&E loop, dedicated lift access via the lobby. The cafeteria below didn't have to be silent — the suite simply couldn't hear it.

3. In‑house joinery, shipped overnight.

Every piece of bespoke joinery — trader desks, library shelving, the 18m client reception desk — was fabricated in our Ubi workshop and trucked in on the Sunday before each phase opened. Mondays began with installation complete and snagging started.
